# Analysis Frameworks

This document provides expanded frameworks for each analysis lens in the Assumption Challenger workflow.

## Framework 1: Hidden Assumptions Detector

### Categories of Assumptions to Probe

**Context Assumptions**
- What environment or ecosystem is assumed stable?
- What resources (time, budget, skills, infrastructure) are assumed available?
- What external dependencies (APIs, vendors, regulations) are assumed reliable?

**User/Audience Assumptions**
- What behaviors, skills, or motivations are assumed?
- What access (internet, devices, literacy) is assumed?
- What cultural or demographic constants are assumed?

**Technical Assumptions**
- What tools, libraries, or platforms are assumed available or compatible?
- What scale, performance, or reliability characteristics are assumed?
- What data quality, structure, or availability is assumed?

**Market/Domain Assumptions**
- What competitive landscape or market conditions are assumed?
- What regulatory or legal environment is assumed?
- What timing or urgency is assumed?

**Process Assumptions**
- What workflows or approval chains are assumed functional?
- What communication channels or collaboration patterns are assumed?
- What iteration speed or feedback loops are assumed?

### Assumption Risk Matrix

For each identified assumption, assess:
- **Criticality**: How much does success depend on this assumption holding true? (High/Medium/Low)
- **Fragility**: How likely is this assumption to be violated? (High/Medium/Low)
- **Observability**: How easy is it to detect if this assumption breaks? (Easy/Moderate/Hard)

Flag assumptions that are High Criticality + High Fragility + Hard Observability as **critical risks**.

## Framework 2: Blind Spot Illuminator

### Types of Blind Spots

**Information Gaps**
- What data is absent or incomplete?
- What metrics are missing to evaluate success?
- What research or validation hasn't been done?

**Structural Blind Spots**
- What systemic constraints aren't visible from this vantage point?
- What organizational politics or incentives create distortions?
- What power dynamics or equity issues are overlooked?

**Temporal Blind Spots**
- What happens at scale (10x, 100x current state)?
- What happens over time (3 months, 1 year, 5 years)?
- What are the second-order and third-order effects?

**Boundary Conditions**
- What happens at the extremes (edge cases, outliers)?
- What happens when assumptions break?
- What happens in crisis or degraded modes?

**Cognitive Blind Spots**
- What confirmation bias might be present?
- What sunk cost fallacy might be operating?
- What survivorship bias might skew perspective?

### Uncertainty Calibration

Classify uncertainties by type:
- **Reducible uncertainty**: Can be resolved with research, testing, or data collection
- **Irreducible uncertainty**: Fundamental unknowns that require adaptive strategies
- **Known unknowns**: Recognized gaps with clear paths to resolution
- **Unknown unknowns**: Unrecognized risks requiring broad scenario planning

## Framework 3: Alternate Viewpoint Generator

### Perspective Categories

**Adversarial Viewpoints**
- What would a competitor exploit?
- What would a bad actor misuse?
- What would a skeptical investor question?
- What would a critical reviewer attack?

**Optimistic Viewpoints**
- What breakthrough could accelerate this 10x?
- What unexpected synergy might emerge?
- What tailwind could amplify impact?
- What is the best-case scenario if all stars align?

**Domain Expert Angles**
- What would a UX designer prioritize differently?
- What would a security expert red-flag?
- What would an operations/scaling expert warn about?
- What would a legal/compliance expert require?
- What would a data scientist question about methodology?

**Stakeholder Perspectives**
- What would end-users value differently?
- What would internal champions need to justify this?
- What would skeptical team members resist?
- What would external partners require?

**Temporal Perspectives**
- What would "past you" 6 months ago have thought of this?
- What will "future you" 1 year from now wish you had considered?
- What would a historian analyzing this in 10 years notice?

**Unconventional Angles**
- What analogies from other domains apply?
- What if core assumptions were inverted?
- What if this were 10x more ambitious? 10x more constrained?
- What would someone with radically different values prioritize?

### Perspective Depth Levels

For each viewpoint:
1. **Surface**: What would they immediately notice?
2. **Structural**: What deeper patterns would they recognize?
3. **Philosophical**: What worldview differences would shape their critique?

## Framework 4: Contradiction Mapper

### Types of Contradictions

**Goal-Method Contradictions**
- Does the proposed approach actually achieve the stated goal?
- Do tactical choices undermine strategic objectives?
- Do short-term optimizations create long-term problems?

**Value Contradictions**
- Where do competing values create tension (e.g., innovation vs. stability)?
- Where do ethical principles conflict with pragmatic considerations?
- Where do individual incentives misalign with collective goals?

**Theory-Practice Gaps**
- Where does ideal behavior diverge from real behavior?
- Where do formal processes differ from actual workflows?
- Where does documented strategy differ from lived execution?

**Structure-Culture Contradictions**
- Does the organizational structure enable or constrain the desired culture?
- Do processes require behaviors that values discourage?
- Do incentives reward actions that principles condemn?

**Resource Contradictions**
- Are timelines realistic given resource constraints?
- Do quality expectations match investment levels?
- Does scope match capacity?

### Productive vs. Fatal Contradictions

**Productive Tensions** (can generate breakthroughs)
- Explore the friction for insights
- Design systems that embrace both poles
- Create dynamic balance mechanisms

**Fatal Contradictions** (require resolution)
- Prioritize one value over the other
- Redesign to eliminate the conflict
- Explicitly acknowledge the tradeoff

## Framework 5: Meta-Analysis Lens

### AI Analysis Limitations

**Pattern Recognition Limits**
- AI excels at: Identifying common patterns, generating alternatives, structured reasoning
- AI struggles with: Novel contexts, tacit knowledge, political dynamics, emotional intelligence

**Knowledge Boundaries**
- Cutoff date constraints (knowledge through January 2025)
- Domain depth limitations (general vs. specialized expertise)
- Real-time information gaps (current market conditions, breaking news)

**Bias Sources in AI Analysis**
- Training data biases (Western-centric, tech-centric, English-centric perspectives)
- Optimization biases (favoring articulate, structured responses over messy reality)
- Anthropomorphization risks (attributing human judgment where pattern-matching operates)

### Human Judgment Requirements

Flag when these are needed:
- **Lived experience**: Understanding cultural nuances, organizational politics, personal relationships
- **Ethical judgment**: Navigating moral gray zones, stakeholder tradeoffs
- **Intuition**: Pattern recognition from years of domain-specific practice
- **Emotional intelligence**: Reading between the lines, assessing morale, gauging sentiment
- **Strategic taste**: Judging when "good enough" is sufficient vs. when perfection matters

### Confidence Calibration Guidelines

**High Confidence (80-95%)**
- Well-documented best practices
- Clear logical contradictions
- Common failure patterns
- Established research findings

**Medium Confidence (50-80%)**
- Domain-specific but generalizable insights
- Reasonable extrapolations from known patterns
- Educated guesses based on partial information

**Low Confidence (<50%)**
- Speculative scenarios
- Context-dependent judgments
- Areas requiring specialized domain expertise
- Novel or unprecedented situations

## Framework 6: Anti-Paralysis Safeguards

### Preventing Over-Analysis

**Red Flags for Analysis Paralysis**
- Analysis produces more questions than answers
- Uncertainty increases rather than decreases with investigation
- Refinement iterations show diminishing returns
- No clear path to "done"

**Simplification Strategies**
1. **90% Solution Rule**: Identify the 10% of effort that yields 90% of value
2. **Minimal Viable Analysis**: What's the least analysis needed to make a defensible decision?
3. **Timeboxing**: Set fixed analysis duration; decide with available information
4. **Reversibility Check**: Is this decision reversible? If yes, optimize for speed over perfection

### Decision Forcing Mechanisms

**At the end of analysis, always provide:**
1. **If you had to decide today**: What would you do with current information?
2. **Minimum info needed**: What's the one piece of missing data that matters most?
3. **Stop-loss condition**: Under what condition should you abandon this path?
4. **Next best action**: What concrete next step moves this forward?

## Framework 7: Prompt Engineering Specific Analysis

For AI prompts, use this specialized framework:

### Prompt Anatomy Checklist

**Clarity**
- Is the task/goal explicitly stated?
- Are success criteria defined?
- Are constraints specified?
- Is the desired output format clear?

**Context**
- Is necessary background provided?
- Are relevant examples included (few-shot)?
- Is domain-specific terminology explained?
- Is the intended audience/use case stated?

**Guidance**
- Is reasoning approach suggested (chain-of-thought, step-by-step)?
- Are edge cases addressed?
- Are quality standards defined?
- Are error handling instructions included?

**Constraints**
- Are token/length limits specified?
- Are style guidelines provided?
- Are safety/ethical boundaries defined?
- Are prohibited approaches listed?

### Model-Specific Considerations

**Claude Sonnet 4.5**
- Leverage extended context window (200K tokens) for comprehensive examples
- Use structured thinking prompts for complex reasoning
- Specify output format preferences (markdown, code blocks, artifacts)
- Utilize tool-calling for computational tasks

**GPT-5** (anticipated capabilities)
- Assume advanced reasoning and multi-step planning
- Leverage improved math and logical reasoning
- Consider video/multimodal capabilities if applicable
- Use system message for persona/role framing

**Grok 4** (anticipated capabilities)
- Assume real-time web access; specify data freshness requirements
- Consider conversational/casual tone calibration
- Use when real-time information is critical

### Common Prompt Weaknesses

**Ambiguity**
- Underspecified goals leading to varied interpretations
- Vague quality standards ("make it good")
- Unclear scope boundaries

**Over/Under-Constraint**
- Over-constraint: Stifles creativity, produces generic outputs
- Under-constraint: Produces off-target or inconsistent results

**Missing Scaffolding**
- No examples when task is non-standard
- No reasoning guidance for complex problems
- No output format specification

**Assumption Failures**
- Assumes model capabilities beyond limits (real-time data, perfect math)
- Assumes model knowledge currency (post-cutoff events)
- Assumes model reads intent vs. explicit instructions

### Optimization Dimensions

1. **Clarity**: Can this be misinterpreted? Add constraints.
2. **Efficiency**: Is this unnecessarily verbose? Tighten language.
3. **Robustness**: Will this handle edge cases? Add examples.
4. **Adaptability**: Is this too rigid? Add flexibility parameters.
5. **Evaluability**: Can output quality be objectively assessed? Add criteria.
