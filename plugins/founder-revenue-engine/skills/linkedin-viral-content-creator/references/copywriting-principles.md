# Copywriting Principles for Viral Hooks

## Core Principles

### 1. Pattern Interrupt
Break the scroll with unexpected statements, contrarian takes, or format disruptions that stop autopilot scrolling.

**Techniques:**
- Start with "I disagree" or "Warning"
- Use unexpected numbers or statistics
- Challenge conventional wisdom
- Create visual or linguistic disruption

### 2. Curiosity Gap
Open loops that create tension between what the reader knows and what they want to know. The gap must be compelling enough to click/read but not misleading.

**Techniques:**
- Tease specific results without revealing the method
- Use "Here's why" or "Here's how" without immediate payoff
- Reference something counterintuitive or surprising
- Build anticipation with specificity

### 3. Specificity Principle
Concrete details are more believable and engaging than vague claims. Specific numbers, timeframes, and outcomes create credibility.

**Examples:**
- "10X your productivity" vs "From 40 hours to 8 hours per week in 90 days"
- "Save money" vs "Cut costs by 70% using this method"
- "Get results fast" vs "See results in 21 days"

### 4. Social Proof & Authority
Demonstrate credibility through testing, research, client results, or extensive experience.

**Techniques:**
- "I tested 47 tools..."
- "After working with 200+ clients..."
- "Based on 500+ implementations..."
- "My client achieved [specific result]..."

### 5. Emotional Resonance
Tap into core emotions: FOMO, frustration, hope, validation, curiosity, urgency.

**Key emotions for engagement:**
- **FOMO**: "If you don't... you'll miss..."
- **Validation**: "If you're like me and..."
- **Hope**: "I went from [pain] to [pleasure]..."
- **Frustration**: "Most [audience] are wasting..."
- **Curiosity**: "Did you know..."

### 6. The "You" Focus
Make it about the reader, not yourself. Even transformation stories should emphasize the reader's potential outcome.

**Shift from:**
- "I grew my business by 300%" 
**To:**
- "Here's how you can grow your business by 300%"

### 7. Immediate Value Promise
Signal clear benefit within the first 5 words. The reader should know "what's in it for me" instantly.

### 8. Brevity & Punch
First 3-5 words are critical. Each word must earn its place. Cut filler ruthlessly.

## Proven Frameworks

### PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)
1. **Problem**: Identify the pain point
2. **Agitate**: Make them feel the problem more acutely
3. **Solution**: Offer the resolution

**Example**: "Most founders waste 15+ hours weekly on tasks AI could do in minutes. That's 780 hours per year. Here's exactly what to automate first."

### AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)
1. **Attention**: Pattern interrupt or bold claim
2. **Interest**: Deepen engagement with specifics
3. **Desire**: Show transformation or benefit
4. **Action**: Clear next step or payoff

### Before-After-Bridge (BAB)
1. **Before**: Paint the current painful state
2. **After**: Show the desired outcome
3. **Bridge**: Reveal how to get there

**Example**: "I spent 40 hours weekly on content. Now it's 8 hours. This automation framework changed everything."

### The 4 U's Formula
Every great hook should be:
1. **Urgent**: Time-sensitive or important now
2. **Unique**: Different from what they've seen
3. **Ultra-specific**: Concrete details and numbers
4. **Useful**: Clear practical benefit

## Platform Adaptations

### Twitter/X
- **Optimal length**: 100-200 characters for hook
- **Style**: Punchy, contrarian, data-driven
- **Format**: Often standalone statement or thread starter
- **Tone**: Confident, sometimes provocative

### LinkedIn
- **Optimal length**: 150-300 characters for hook
- **Style**: Professional but personal, story-driven
- **Format**: Often personal insight or business transformation
- **Tone**: Authoritative yet relatable, less aggressive than Twitter

### Instagram
- **Optimal length**: 125-200 characters (before "more" cutoff)
- **Style**: Visual-first, lifestyle-oriented, aspirational
- **Format**: Often personal or transformation-focused
- **Tone**: Warm, authentic, inspirational

### TikTok
- **Optimal length**: 80-120 characters (on-screen text)
- **Style**: Fast-paced, trend-aware, pattern interrupt
- **Format**: Question, challenge, or curiosity gap
- **Tone**: Casual, energetic, conversational

### Facebook/Meta
- **Optimal length**: 150-250 characters for hook
- **Style**: Community-focused, relatable, story-driven
- **Format**: Often personal story or group-relevant insight
- **Tone**: Conversational, friendly, accessible

## Hook Quality Criteria

### Essential Elements
1. ✅ **Stops the scroll** - Pattern interrupt or curiosity
2. ✅ **Clear audience** - Speaks to specific ICP
3. ✅ **Value promise** - Benefit is obvious
4. ✅ **Specificity** - Concrete details, not vague claims
5. ✅ **Emotional trigger** - Engages feeling, not just logic

### Red Flags to Avoid
- ❌ Generic claims without specifics
- ❌ Clickbait that doesn't deliver
- ❌ Too long before getting to the point
- ❌ Unclear who it's for
- ❌ No clear benefit or payoff
- ❌ Overused phrases ("game-changer," "must-read")
- ❌ Burying the lead past first line

## Hook Enhancement Techniques

### Strengthen Weak Hooks
1. **Add specificity**: Replace vague with concrete
2. **Create contrast**: Before/after, with/without
3. **Inject urgency**: Add timeframe or FOMO
4. **Increase stakes**: Bigger numbers or outcomes
5. **Sharpen focus**: Remove unnecessary words

### Generate Variations
When creating multiple hook options:
1. **Different frameworks**: Try PAS, BAB, curiosity gap
2. **Different emotions**: FOMO, validation, frustration
3. **Different angles**: Problem, solution, transformation
4. **Different lengths**: Punchy vs. descriptive
5. **Different formats**: Question, statement, story

### Platform Translation
When adapting a hook across platforms:
1. **Preserve core value** proposition
2. **Adjust tone** to platform norms
3. **Modify length** to platform constraints
4. **Adapt format** to platform expectations
5. **Test variants** for each platform's algorithm
