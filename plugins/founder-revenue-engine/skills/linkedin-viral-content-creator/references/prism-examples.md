# PRISM Examples

Before/after examples demonstrating PRISM humanization framework application.

## PRISM Framework Reminder

- **P**attern break: Imperfection markers (no perfect grammar)
- **R**hythm: Vary sentence length (short. punchy. long flowing thoughts)
- **I**mperfection: Personality quirks, lowercase emphasis
- **S**tart mid-thought: Drop into insight directly
- **M**eta: Self-aware commentary when appropriate

## Example 1: Remote Work Career Ceiling

**Before (Tail Angle):**
"Your boss promotes people they see. If you're fully remote, you're structurally capped at 'individual contributor' forever because creative leadership requires presence politics."

**After (PRISM Applied):**
"hard truth: if i can't see you, i probably won't promote you. we call it 'remote flexibility' but it's actually a career ceiling for creatives. all the senior roles? they're back in the office playing politics."

**PRISM breakdown:**
- **P** (Pattern break): Dropped capitalization, sentence fragments
- **R** (Rhythm): Short declarative. Question mark pause. Longer explanation.
- **I** (Imperfection): "hard truth:" casual phrasing, lowercase "i"
- **S** (Start mid-thought): Opens with conclusion, not setup
- **M** (Meta): "we call it X but it's Y" frame

## Example 2: Async Work Reality

**Before (Tail Angle):**
"Remote work isn't 'freedom.' It's a shift from paid time to unpaid anxiety because creative work never actually clocks out when feedback comes at 11 PM across time zones."

**After (PRISM Applied):**
"everyone celebrates remote work until they realize creative feedback now arrives at 11pm across three time zones. you didn't escape the office. you just made your bedroom the 24/7 war room."

**PRISM breakdown:**
- **P** (Pattern break): "everyone celebrates until" opens unconventionally
- **R** (Rhythm): Long setup sentence. Two short punches.
- **I** (Imperfection): Lowercase throughout, "11pm" no space, casual "you didn't"
- **S** (Start mid-thought): Opens with others' behavior, not explanation
- **M** (Meta): "war room" reframe is self-aware metaphor

## Example 3: Real Estate Arbitrage

**Before (Tail Angle):**
"Companies don't care about your flexibility. They care that they offloaded $15k/year of office rent onto your personal mortgage while keeping headcount flat."

**After (PRISM Applied):**
"stopped calling it 'work from home' and started calling it 'subsidizing my employer's real estate costs.' they saved $15k/year per person in rent. i now pay for their downsizing."

**PRISM breakdown:**
- **P** (Pattern break): Opens with personal reframing action
- **R** (Rhythm): Long reframe. Medium fact. Short personal conclusion.
- **I** (Imperfection): Lowercase, dropped "I" capitalization, deliberate shift
- **S** (Start mid-thought): Opens with conclusion (the reframe), not premise
- **M** (Meta): Explicit about changing language as intervention

## Example 4: Productivity Theater

**Before (Tail Angle):**
"Remote creative work killed spontaneous ideation. Every conversation now requires a pre-meeting doc, an agenda, a follow-up summary. You spend more time documenting ideas than having them."

**After (PRISM Applied):**
"remote work killed the creative spark. before: walk to someone's desk, sketch on whiteboard, decide in 10 minutes. now: pre-meeting doc, scheduled call, follow-up summary. we document ideas instead of having them."

**PRISM breakdown:**
- **P** (Pattern break): Colon introducing before/after without traditional setup
- **R** (Rhythm): Short claim. Before list (fragments). Now list. Conclusion.
- **I** (Imperfection): "creative spark" (emotional), list fragments, "we" shift
- **S** (Start mid-thought): Opens with outcome, fills in process after
- **M** (Meta): "document ideas instead of having them" is conscious abstraction

## Example 5: Tool Obsession

**Before (Tail Angle):**
"Productivity system design has become its own form of procrastination. People spend more time optimizing their note-taking workflow than actually taking notes or using their knowledge."

**After (PRISM Applied):**
"at some point we started spending more time building the perfect note-taking system than we spend... actually taking notes. weird how 'productivity optimization' became the thing preventing productivity."

**PRISM breakdown:**
- **P** (Pattern break): "at some point" casual temporal vagueness
- **R** (Rhythm): Long first clause with ellipsis pause. Observation punch.
- **I** (Imperfection): Lowercase, ellipsis pause, "weird how" understatement
- **S** (Start mid-thought): Opens in middle of thought process
- **M** (Meta): Explicit observation about the irony ("weird how")

## Example 6: Personal Brand Theater

**Before (Tail Angle):**
"'Building your personal brand' is unpaid marketing labor that creates corporate influencer infrastructure while individuals compete for visibility in oversaturated attention markets."

**After (PRISM Applied):**
"'personal brand' is just unpaid marketing labor with extra steps. you're building infrastructure for LinkedIn while competing with 10,000 others doing the same thing. they monetize the attention economy. you get a profile."

**PRISM breakdown:**
- **P** (Pattern break): "with extra steps" meme reference, casual
- **R** (Rhythm): Short definition. Medium context. Short asymmetry statement. Punch.
- **I** (Imperfection): Lowercase, "you're/you get" direct address, fragments
- **S** (Start mid-thought): Opens with redefinition, not explanation
- **M** (Meta): Calling out the asymmetry explicitly

## Rhythm Patterns

**Short. Medium. Longer flowing thought.**
Example: "hard truth: remote work sold us freedom. what we got was isolation packaged as flexibility and a career ceiling nobody mentions until you hit it."

**Long setup, short punch, short punch.**
Example: "everyone celebrates async collaboration until they realize design by committee over Slack means nobody fights for good ideas anymore. beige consensus. death by thread."

**Question setup, answer subversion.**
Example: "want to know why your productivity system isn't working? you're solving the wrong problem. the bottleneck isn't organization. it's that you're organizing to avoid doing the actual hard work."

## Imperfection Markers

**Lowercase for emphasis:**
- "i" instead of "I" (makes it feel personal, not formal)
- Starting sentences lowercase (breaks formality)
- Proper nouns can stay capped (Slack, LinkedIn)

**Fragments:**
- "Death by documentation."
- "Beige consensus."
- "War room architecture."

**Ellipsis pauses:**
- "we spend more time... actually doing the thing"
- "and then you realize... nobody's getting promoted"

**Contractions:**
- "you're" not "you are"
- "didn't" not "did not"
- "it's" not "it is"

## Meta-Commentary Patterns

**Reframing language:**
- "stopped calling it X and started calling it Y"
- "we call it X but it's actually Y"
- "they say X, what they mean is Y"

**Noticing patterns:**
- "weird how..."
- "interesting that..."
- "notice how..."

**Calling out irony:**
- "the thing meant to [goal] now [opposite]"
- "[process] designed to enable [X] actually prevents [X]"

**Economic revelation:**
- "who's really paying?"
- "who captures the value here?"
- "follow the money"

## Starting Mid-Thought Techniques

**Open with the reframe:**
"stopped calling it 'productivity' and started calling it 'procrastination with better tools.'"

**Open with the conclusion:**
"remote work didn't give us freedom. it just moved the office into our bedrooms."

**Open with direct address:**
"you think you're building a personal brand. you're actually building LinkedIn's content moat."

**Open with observation:**
"everyone's optimizing their note-taking system. nobody's shipping."

**Open with the economic truth:**
"companies saved $15k per employee in office costs. you now subsidize their real estate savings."

## Quality Checks

**Good PRISM application:**
- ✓ Sounds like human wrote it, not AI
- ✓ Has personality and voice
- ✓ Rhythm varies naturally
- ✓ Imperfections feel authentic
- ✓ Gets to point quickly
- ✓ Meta-commentary adds insight

**Bad PRISM application:**
- ✗ Lowercase everywhere (forced, not selective)
- ✗ Every sentence same length
- ✗ Fragments without purpose
- ✗ Meta-commentary feels performative
- ✗ Too many stylistic quirks (trying too hard)
- ✗ Still sounds like AI attempting casualness

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

**Don't:**
- Use ellipsis in every sentence (becomes tic)
- Make everything lowercase (loses impact)
- Fragment everything (loses coherence)
- Add "interesting..." to everything (forced meta)
- Use "here's the thing" (banned phrase)
- Start with "Let's talk about" (instant AI tell)

**Do:**
- Mix formal and casual strategically
- Save fragments for impact moments
- Use rhythm to control pace
- Make meta-commentary reveal structure
- Let personality emerge naturally
- Start with insight, not setup

## Practice Examples

**Tail Angle:** "Side projects are marketed as career accelerators but actually signal that your main job doesn't challenge you, making you look uncommitted to employers."

**PRISM Version:** "side projects aren't career boosters. they're signals that your day job is boring and you're already looking elsewhere. employers see: 'this person's checked out.' not a great look when promotion time comes."

**Tail Angle:** "Continuous learning advice ignores that most skills have zero return on investment because credential inflation means everyone has the same certifications."

**PRISM Version:** "everyone says 'keep learning' but nobody mentions that when everyone has the same certifications, you just spent $500 and 40 hours to stay exactly where you were. credential inflation is real and it's expensive."
