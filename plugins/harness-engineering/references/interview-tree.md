# Harness Interview Tree

Resolve branches in dependency order. Ask one to three related questions at a time.

1. Platform: Claude Code, Claude Cowork, Codex, or several. Several means separate harness scopes.
2. Outcome: what becomes easier, safer, faster, or more consistent.
3. Users: who operates the harness and who reviews its output.
4. Work: recurring tasks, deliverables, projects, and source systems.
5. Scope: global defaults, workspace rules, project rules, and exclusions.
6. Files: current config home or connected folders, workspaces, examples, templates, and output destinations.
7. Capabilities: skills, plugins, connectors, MCP servers, automation surfaces, and optional bundles the platform offers.
8. Authority: read, write, install, authenticate, publish, spend, delete, and external actions.
9. Quality: deterministic checks, judgment criteria, reviewers, and evidence surfaces.
10. Continuity: memory, autonomous runs, resume behavior, state files, schedules, and notifications. On Cowork, where durable state lives outside the session.
11. Maintenance: ownership, drift checks, update cadence, and model-change review.

Record each answer as a fact, constraint, preference, assumption, or decision. Close a branch only when its acceptance test and governing scope are clear.
