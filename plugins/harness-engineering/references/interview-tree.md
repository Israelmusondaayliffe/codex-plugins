# Harness Interview Tree

Resolve branches in dependency order. Ask one to three related questions at a time.

1. Outcome: what becomes easier, safer, faster, or more consistent.
2. Users: who operates the harness and who reviews its output.
3. Work: recurring tasks, deliverables, projects, and source systems.
4. Scope: global defaults, workspace rules, project rules, and exclusions.
5. Files: current Codex home, workspaces, examples, templates, and output destinations.
6. Capabilities: skills, plugins, connectors, Browser, Computer Use, CLI, IDE, and desktop.
7. Authority: read, write, install, authenticate, publish, spend, delete, and external actions.
8. Quality: deterministic checks, judgment criteria, reviewers, and evidence surfaces.
9. Continuity: memory, Goals, resume behavior, state files, schedules, and notifications.
10. Maintenance: ownership, drift checks, update cadence, and model-change review.

Record each answer as a fact, constraint, preference, assumption, or decision. Close a branch only when its acceptance test and governing scope are clear.
