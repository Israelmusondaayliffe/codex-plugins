# Creative Directions for Brand Design

Brand-specific creative starters organized by transformation axis. Use during Phase 3 to generate diverse directions and during Phase 5 to differentiate Prompt B (creative push) from Prompt A (clean execution).

---

## Axis 1: Material Contrasts

The most reliable brand design lever. Take the brand's signature material and either apply it to something unexpected, or replace it with something jarring.

**Luxury material on mundane object**:
- Chanel bouclé tweed on a toolbox
- Louis Vuitton monogram canvas on a fire extinguisher
- Tiffany sterling silver on a stapler
- Ferrari carbon fiber on a lunch tray

**Industrial material on luxury object**:
- Supreme box logo on concrete blocks
- Monster Energy neon green on surgical instruments
- IKEA birch plywood on a haute couture dress form
- Red Bull aluminum on a jewelry box

**Natural material on tech object**:
- Patagonia recycled fleece wrapping a drone
- Muji kraft cardboard as laptop shell
- Lacoste pique cotton on headphone cups
- The North Face Gore-Tex on a smart speaker

**Material inversion** (the brand's own material, wrong context):
- Nike Flyknit mesh on furniture
- Apple aluminum as kitchenware
- Adidas Boost foam as architectural material
- Tesla glass roof panel as a serving tray

---

## Axis 2: Context Shifts

Move the brand to an environment, era, or culture it has never existed in.

**Temporal shifts**:
- Brand reimagined for 1920s Art Deco era
- Brand as it would have existed in ancient Rome
- Brand in a cyberpunk 2077 future
- Brand's founding-era aesthetic applied to modern products
- Brand as a Victorian-era catalogue illustration

**Geographic/cultural shifts**:
- Brand reimagined through Japanese wabi-sabi philosophy
- Brand as Moroccan souk market stall
- Brand in Scandinavian hygge context
- Brand as Soviet-era propaganda poster
- Brand as Edo-period woodblock print

**Industry shifts**:
- Fashion brand as automotive manufacturer
- Tech brand as restaurant/hospitality
- Sports brand as fine art gallery
- Energy drink brand as luxury spa
- Furniture brand as haute couture house

---

## Axis 3: Scale Plays

Shift the scale of the brand's visual language to create surprise.

**Miniature brand worlds**:
- Brand HQ as a detailed architectural model/diorama
- Brand products as dollhouse-scale objects
- Brand logo as a tiny jeweler's piece worn as brooch
- Capsule collection at 1/12 scale on miniature shelves

**Monumental brand monuments**:
- Brand logo as Brutalist concrete monument in public plaza
- Brand product scaled to building-size in urban landscape
- Brand name carved into a mountainside
- Brand bottle as a water tower

**Macro extremes**:
- Extreme close-up on brand's signature texture filling entire frame
- Brand logo at microscopic level revealing hidden material structure
- Single thread of brand fabric at 100x magnification

---

## Axis 4: Typography Treatments

Rethink how the brand name appears visually.

**Historical type treatments**:
- American retro baseball script (UC 8.1)
- Swiss International Style grid poster (UC 8.3)
- Art Nouveau flowing organic letterforms
- Bauhaus geometric construction
- Psychedelic 1960s poster lettering

**Physical type treatments**:
- Brand name as neon sign (UC 3.2)
- Brand name embossed in wax seal (UC 2.7)
- Brand name carved in stone/marble
- Brand name formed from food/ingredients
- Brand name written in smoke/vapor
- Brand name as topiary garden hedge

**Digital/motion type treatments**:
- Frame-by-frame assembly animation (UC 8.2)
- Glitch/corrupted digital text
- Holographic projection floating in space
- ASCII art formation of brand name
- QR code that forms the brand name shape

---

## Axis 5: Logo Transformations

The logo itself becomes the creative canvas.

**Material transformations** (what the logo is made of):
- Glass (UC 2.3)
- Dark metallic chrome (UC 2.6)
- Wax seal (UC 2.7)
- Ice/frozen (UC 3.4)
- Moss/living plants (UC 3.5)
- Liquid metal (UC 3.6)
- Retextured with candy, fabric, circuit boards (UC 2.8)

**Context transformations** (where/how the logo appears):
- Formed by crowds from above (UC 2.4)
- Sticker-bombed 3D object (UC 2.10)
- Embossed blind contour (UC 2.5)
- Neon sign on brick wall (UC 3.2)
- Inflatable sculpture (UC 3.3)
- Gallery sculpture (UC 3.1)

**Unconventional logo contexts** (not in catalog, for creative push prompts):
- Logo reflected in a puddle on a rainy street
- Logo shadow cast by sunlight through a window cutout
- Logo formed by negative space in a crowd scene
- Logo visible only under UV blacklight
- Logo imprinted in sand being washed away by ocean wave
- Logo formed by the arrangement of products on a shelf

## Axis 6: Product Category Jumps

The core of brand recontextualization. Move the brand into product categories it has never touched.

**Fashion brand → Kitchen**:
- Chanel knife set, Balenciaga mixing bowls, Off-White cutting board

**Tech brand → Sport**:
- Apple boxing gloves, Tesla tennis racket, Google climbing gear

**Auto brand → Beverage**:
- Porsche coffee maker, Ferrari wine bottle, BMW flask

**Sports brand → Home**:
- Nike desk lamp, Adidas bookshelf, Under Armour door handles

**Luxury brand → Utility**:
- Tiffany hammer, Louis Vuitton toolbox, Gucci fire extinguisher

**Food/Drink brand → Fashion**:
- Red Bull high heels, Monster Energy evening gown, Coca-Cola leather jacket

**The freshness test** (from UC 1.5): "Would Highsnobiety or Hypebeast repost this?" If no, make it weirder.

---

## Axis 7: Presentation Format Shifts

Change how the output is structured, not just what's in it.

**Single hero product** (standard):
- One object, studio background, UI overlays (UC 1.1, 1.2, 1.4, 1.7)

**Capsule collection / knolling**:
- Multiple objects arranged in flat-lay grid (UC 1.3)

**Bento grid / brand system**:
- 6-module layout with hero, social, palette, type, logo, manifesto (UC 2.1)

**Campaign grid**:
- Asymmetric grid with logo blocks, slogan blocks, photography (UC 4.5)

**Poster format**:
- Fragmented portrait (UC 4.4), sport poster (UC 4.2), Swiss type (UC 8.3)

**Icon/asset set**:
- 9-sticker grid (UC 5.3), duotone icons (UC 5.4)

**Editorial sequence**:
- Frame-by-frame animation strip (UC 8.2)

**Format jumps for creative push**:
- Take a use case normally shown as single hero product, present it as capsule collection
- Take a logo treatment normally shown in isolation, present it as campaign poster
- Take a brand kit bento grid, present it as a physical printed poster mockup pinned to a wall
- Take a product concept, present it as an Instagram carousel mockup
