# Brand DNA Extraction Framework

Use this reference during Phase 1 to analyze any brand's visual DNA. Extract these attributes to inform prompt generation.

## Extraction Framework

For any brand, identify:

1. **Primary colors**: The 1-2 dominant brand colors
2. **Secondary colors**: Supporting palette colors
3. **Signature materials**: Materials the brand is known for
4. **Iconic patterns/textures**: Visual patterns associated with the brand
5. **Logo style**: Shape, complexity, wordmark vs. symbol
6. **Typography**: Font family/style the brand uses
7. **Design philosophy**: Minimalist, maximalist, functional, decorative, etc.
8. **Heritage era**: When the brand's visual identity was established or feels rooted
9. **Target audience**: Who the brand speaks to
10. **Price positioning**: Mass-market, premium, luxury, ultra-luxury
11. **Cultural associations**: What the brand evokes beyond its products

### 1. Nike
- Primary: Black, white
- Secondary: Volt green, university red, royal blue
- Materials: Flyknit mesh, Air unit TPU, rubber, recycled polyester
- Patterns: Swoosh silhouette, dynamic motion lines
- Logo: Swoosh. Minimal, universally recognized
- Typography: Futura Condensed Extra Bold
- Philosophy: Performance-driven minimalism. "Just Do It" ethos.
- Heritage: 1970s Oregon track culture
- Audience: Athletes, streetwear, aspirational fitness
- Positioning: Premium mass-market
- Associations: Competition, movement, personal achievement, Michael Jordan

## Material Vocabulary by Brand Tier

**Ultra-Luxury** (Chanel, LV, Ferrari, Tiffany):
Exotic leathers (crocodile, ostrich), 18k+ gold, platinum, hand-stitched details, haute couture textiles, precious stones, lacquered finishes, Alcantara, silk

**Luxury** (Jacquemus, Balenciaga, Off-White, Porsche):
Full-grain leather, carbon fiber, anodized aluminum, technical fabrics, deconstructed raw edges, industrial hardware, brushed metals, matte ceramics

**Premium** (Nike, Adidas, TNF, Apple, Tesla):
Aerospace-grade aluminum, Flyknit/Primeknit technical mesh, Gore-Tex, surgical-grade steel, precision-milled CNC parts, recycled technical fabrics, borosilicate glass

**Premium Casual** (Lacoste, Patagonia):
Pique cotton, organic cotton, recycled polyester, Fair Trade certified materials, natural dyes, raw leather, canvas

**Mass-Market** (IKEA, Muji, Monster, Red Bull):
Birch plywood, recycled pulp, kraft cardboard, matte aluminum, injection-molded plastic, unbleached cotton, particleboard

**Streetwear** (Supreme, Off-White crossover):
Heavy cotton (fleece, jersey), industrial zip ties, PVC, raw denim, rubber, screen-printed graphics, enamel pins

## Color Psychology for Brand Contexts

When the engine assigns use cases, consider how brand colors interact with the output:

- **Red brands** (Ferrari, Supreme, Monster): high energy, urgency. Work well with dark/black backgrounds for contrast.
- **Blue brands** (IKEA, Red Bull, Tiffany): trust, calm, premium. Work well with white or warm contrast.
- **Black/white brands** (Nike, Apple, Chanel, Off-White): versatile. Can adapt to any context. Best for material-focused prompts where the tactile quality carries the brand identity.
- **Green brands** (Lacoste, Monster): nature or energy depending on shade. Work well with earth tones or black.
- **Earth/neutral brands** (Muji, Patagonia, Jacquemus): warmth, authenticity. Work well with natural materials and outdoor contexts.
