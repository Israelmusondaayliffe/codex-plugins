# Effects Vocabulary

Named effects for shot-by-shot video prompts. Use these precise terms in BUILD and REMIX outputs. Describe the visual result, not the editing software technique.

## Speed Manipulation

**Speed ramp (acceleration)**: Smooth increase from slow-motion to normal or fast speed. Describe the start/end speed. "Footage accelerates from approximately 30% speed to real-time over 2 seconds."

**Speed ramp (deceleration)**: Smooth decrease from fast to slow. "The action decelerates from full speed to approximately 20% speed, freezing the impact moment."

**Slow-motion (deep)**: Sustained reduced speed, typically 15-25% of real-time. Specify the percentage. "Approximately 20% speed captures micro-expressions and fabric movement."

**Slow-motion (subtle)**: Slight reduction, 50-75% of real-time. "Footage plays at roughly 60% speed, adding weight without feeling obviously slowed."

**Speed freeze**: Complete stop on a single frame, then resume. "Motion freezes for 0.5 seconds on the peak of the jump, then resumes at full speed."

**Reverse speed**: Footage plays backward. "The shattered glass reassembles in reverse at 50% speed."

**Time-lapse compression**: Hours compressed into seconds. "Clouds race across the sky as shadows sweep the ground in compressed time."

## Camera Movement

**Whip pan**: Extremely fast horizontal camera rotation creating motion blur. Specify direction. "Camera whip-pans left-to-right, smearing the frame into horizontal blur."

**Push-in / dolly forward**: Camera moves toward the subject. "Camera pushes in steadily toward the subject's face, closing from medium to tight framing."

**Pull-back / dolly out**: Camera retreats from the subject. "Camera pulls back slowly, revealing the full environment around the subject."

**Crane up / jib rise**: Camera rises vertically. "Camera lifts from ground level to overhead, revealing the landscape below."

**Crane down / jib drop**: Camera descends vertically. "Camera drops from a high angle down to eye level."

**Tracking shot (lateral)**: Camera moves parallel to subject. "Camera tracks alongside the subject at matched speed, maintaining profile framing."

**Orbit / arc**: Camera circles the subject. "Camera arcs 180 degrees around the subject, shifting from front to back view."

**Steadicam float**: Smooth, slightly organic handheld movement. "Camera floats at waist height with subtle organic sway."

**Dutch angle (static)**: Tilted horizon, held. Specify degrees. "Frame tilted approximately 20 degrees clockwise, creating unease."

**Dutch angle (dynamic)**: Horizon tilts during the shot. "Frame rotates from level to approximately 15 degrees counterclockwise over 2 seconds."

## Digital Effects

**Digital zoom (scale-in)**: Post-production scale increase simulating a zoom. "The frame scales inward rapidly, punching toward the subject's face."

**Digital zoom (scale-out)**: Post-production scale decrease. "The frame gradually widens, pulling back from the subject."

**Zoom pump**: Rapid scale-in then snap-back. "Quick scale pulse. The frame punches in then immediately settles back."

**Frame rotation**: Entire image rotates on its center axis. Specify direction and degrees. "The frame rotates clockwise approximately 15 degrees during the shot."

**Mirror / symmetry**: Frame duplicated along an axis. "Vertical mirror along center axis, creating kaleidoscopic symmetry."

**Camera shake / vibration**: High-frequency jitter overlaid on footage. "Post-production camera vibration at high frequency, adding raw energy."

**Lens distortion pulse**: Brief warping of the frame edges. "Wide-angle barrel distortion pulses outward then snaps back."

**Glitch / data corruption**: Brief digital artifact overlay. "Frame tears horizontally with RGB channel separation for 3 frames."

**Multi-exposure / stroboscopic clone**: Multiple instances of the subject composited at different time positions. "8+ instances of the subject at sequential positions, opacity building from 30% to fully opaque."

**Split screen**: Frame divided into panels showing different content. "Frame splits into two vertical panels showing simultaneous perspectives."

**Overlay / double exposure**: Two image layers blended. "Subject's silhouette filled with a landscape texture at 60% opacity."

## Transitions

**White bloom flash**: Overexposed white flash between shots. "Frame blows out to pure white with faint outlines visible through the glow, then resolves into the next shot."

**Motion blur smear**: Heavy directional blur connecting shots. "Frame smears into horizontal motion blur, carrying velocity into the next shot."

**Whip pan transition**: Whip pan at the end of one shot connects to the start of the next. "Shot exits with a right-to-left whip pan. Next shot enters from the same blur direction."

**Fade to black / white**: Gradual opacity change. "Frame fades to black over 1.5 seconds."

**Hard cut**: Instantaneous switch with no transition effect. "Clean cut. No transition. The contrast between shots IS the effect."

**Match cut**: Two shots connected by visual similarity. "Cut from the circular wheel to the circular moon. Same shape, different scale."

**Jump cut**: Same angle with time removed. "Three jump cuts compress the walk to three steps."

**Light flash / strobe**: Brief burst of brightness. "Single-frame white flash punctuates the cut."

## Optical Effects

**Focus pull / rack focus**: Depth-of-field shift between foreground and background. "Focus shifts from the hand in foreground to the face in background over 1 second."

**Bokeh shift**: Background blur character changes. "Background transitions from soft circular bokeh to anamorphic oval streaks."

**Lens flare**: Light artifact from a bright source hitting the lens. "Warm anamorphic flare streaks across the frame from the upper right."

**Anamorphic squeeze**: Horizontal compression characteristic of anamorphic lenses. "Slight anamorphic squeeze on highlights creates oval light shapes."

**Vignette**: Darkening at frame edges. "Heavy vignette isolates the subject in a pool of light."

## Atmospheric Effects

**Particle overlay**: Dust, rain, snow, embers floating in frame. "Fine dust particles catch sidelight, drifting slowly through the frame."

**Volumetric light / god rays**: Visible light beams through atmosphere. "Shafts of warm light cut through haze at approximately 45 degrees."

**Haze / atmosphere**: General atmospheric diffusion. "Thin haze softens the background and creates depth separation."

**Smoke / fog**: Dense atmospheric element. "Low-lying fog rolls across the ground at ankle height."

## Color / Grade Effects

**Color flash**: Brief shift in color grade. "Frame pulses warm amber for 3 frames then returns to neutral."

**Desaturation sweep**: Color drains from the image. "Saturation drops from full color to near-monochrome over 2 seconds."

**High contrast crush**: Blacks deepen, highlights blow. "Aggressive contrast crushes shadows to pure black, leaving only highlights."

**Cross-process / color shift**: Unusual color mapping. "Teal shadows with warm orange highlights. Split-tone grade."
