# Vault Miner (Move 3)

Research synthesis is where extraction runs deepest. The output is not a report, it is a
vault: atomized, linked, one insight per note, readable as context by every future session
and every future model.

## Workflow

1. Run or collect the deep research: the user's niche, competitors, customer problems,
   methods they keep meaning to study. If research already exists (reports, threads,
   transcripts), mine it instead of redoing it.
2. Atomize. Each note: one insight, a title that states the insight as a claim, 3 to 10
   lines of body, source, and links to related notes. Use
   `assets/learnings-note-template.md`.
3. Link deliberately. A note with no links is an orphan and will never be resurfaced.
   When writing note N, search existing notes for its neighbors and link both ways.
4. Deliver the vault as a folder of markdown files compatible with Obsidian or any linked
   notes tool, plus a short index note per topic cluster. The vault is a living artifact:
   agree its stable location against the workspace contract once, exempt from dated-output
   folders, since links break when the folder moves.

## Why atomize

A hundred linked one-insight notes get retrieved and reused. A 40-page report gets stored
and forgotten. Retrieval, not storage, is the goal.

## Quality check

Pick three random notes. Can each be understood alone, does its title state a claim rather
than a topic, does it link to at least one neighbor? A note titled "Competitor pricing" fails;
"Competitor X wins on onboarding speed, not price" passes.
