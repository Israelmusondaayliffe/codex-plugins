# Executing Knowledge-Work Plans

## Purpose

Carry out an approved plan without losing its constraints or drifting into a different task.

## Workflow

### 1. Load And Review The Plan

Read the complete plan and its brief. Before acting, check for:

- Contradictory tasks or constraints.
- Missing inputs.
- Unclear source ownership.
- Tasks that cannot be verified.
- Approval boundaries.

Resolve blocking problems before execution. Fix small plan defects inline when the intended result is clear and the change does not alter scope.

### 2. Establish Progress State

For long work, maintain a durable progress record beside the plan. Record completed tasks, outputs, checks, open findings, and the next task.

After compaction or resume, trust the artifacts and progress record over recalled conversation history.

### 3. Execute A Coherent Stage

For each task:

1. Load the named inputs.
2. Perform only the planned actions.
3. Create the planned output.
4. Run the task's verification check.
5. Record the result and any limitation.

Do not mark a task complete because effort was spent. Mark it complete when the named evidence surface passes.

### 4. Use Conditional Skills

- Deep research: `knowledge-work-superpowers:systematic-research`
- Claim testing: `knowledge-work-superpowers:evidence-first-analysis`
- Sourced drafting: `knowledge-work-superpowers:drafting-from-evidence`
- Permitted independent research strands: `knowledge-work-superpowers:dispatching-parallel-research`
- Draft review: `knowledge-work-superpowers:reviewing-knowledge-work`

### 5. Check The Plan After New Evidence

Pause and revise the plan when:

- A source of truth contradicts a core assumption.
- Required evidence is unavailable.
- The user's decision changes.
- A planned task no longer answers the brief.
- The work would exceed the approved scope or permissions.

Explain the new fact and its effect. Do not continue mechanically with an invalid plan.

### 6. Complete The Plan

After all tasks pass their checks:

1. Review brief coverage.
2. Review open limitations and unresolved claims.
3. Load `knowledge-work-superpowers:verification-before-delivery`.
4. Load `knowledge-work-superpowers:finishing-a-deliverable` only after verification passes.

## Stop Conditions

Stop and ask for direction when:

- Required authority is missing.
- A meaningful user preference cannot be inferred.
- Sources are inaccessible and no safe substitute exists.
- Several failed attempts reveal that the plan or evidence model is wrong.
- Completion would require a materially different deliverable.
