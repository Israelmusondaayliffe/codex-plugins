# Receiving Work Review

## Purpose

Treat feedback as claims to evaluate, not commands to accept automatically. Correct feedback should improve the work. Incorrect or conflicting feedback should be challenged with evidence.

## Workflow

### 1. Read Everything First

Read the complete review before editing. Group related findings and identify dependencies or contradictions.

Do not revise item by item while later feedback may change the interpretation.

### 2. Restate The Finding

For each item, identify:

- What the reviewer says is wrong or missing.
- Where it applies.
- Which brief requirement or quality standard it invokes.
- Whether the requested change is explicit or inferred.

### 3. Verify Against The Work

Inspect the cited section, sources, claim ledger, and brief. Decide whether the finding is:

- Correct.
- Correct in part.
- Unclear.
- Incorrect.
- In conflict with another binding instruction.

Do not rely on the reviewer's confidence or status alone.

### 4. Resolve Unclear Or Conflicting Feedback

Ask for clarification when the answer changes the result and cannot be discovered. If feedback conflicts with the brief or user instruction, present the conflict and ask which governs.

### 5. Prioritize

Address changes in this order:

1. Factual errors and unsupported claims.
2. Brief or policy violations.
3. Reasoning gaps that affect conclusions.
4. Missing context or counterevidence.
5. Structure and clarity.
6. Minor presentation issues.

### 6. Revise In Small Batches

Make the smallest change that resolves the finding without weakening correct material. Re-check linked claims and citations when one change affects several sections.

### 7. Record The Disposition

For consequential reviews, record each finding as:

- Accepted and fixed.
- Partially accepted and fixed.
- Rejected with evidence.
- Needs user decision.
- Deferred with reason.

### 8. Re-Review

Run the relevant checks again. Do not assume a revision solved the issue or left the rest of the deliverable untouched.

## Pushback

Push back when a suggestion would:

- Introduce an unsupported claim.
- Hide material uncertainty or counterevidence.
- Replace a primary source with a weaker source.
- Violate the approved scope or format.
- Add work that does not serve the decision.
- Conflict with a higher-priority instruction.

Use concise reasoning and point to the evidence. If your pushback is shown to be wrong, correct it plainly and revise.

## Handoff

After all accepted changes are made and open decisions are resolved, load `knowledge-work-superpowers:reviewing-knowledge-work` for the affected areas, then `knowledge-work-superpowers:verification-before-delivery`.
