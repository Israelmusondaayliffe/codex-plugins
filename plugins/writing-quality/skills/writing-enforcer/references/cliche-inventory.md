# Cliche Inventory: Domain-Specific Overused Patterns

This reference organizes commonly overused phrases and patterns by domain for systematic elimination.

## How to Use This Inventory

1. **Identify domain** of output being refined
2. **Load relevant section** below
3. **Scan output** for matches
4. **For each match:**
   - Identify the underlying idea being expressed
   - Find fresh, specific language for that idea
   - Verify replacement isn't another cliche
5. **Final pass** to catch any new cliches introduced

## Business Writing

### Strategy & Planning
**Overused:**
- "align stakeholders"
- "create synergies"
- "leverage existing assets"
- "low-hanging fruit"
- "move the needle"
- "table stakes"
- "shift left" (in any metaphorical sense)
- "boil the ocean"
- "drink our own champagne"
- "put a pin in that"
- "parking lot that idea"

**Fresh alternatives (examples):**
- Instead of "align stakeholders" → "get sales and engineering to agree on priorities"
- Instead of "low-hanging fruit" → "the 3 features we can ship this month"
- Instead of "move the needle" → "increase conversion by 2%"

### Marketing & Growth
**Overused:**
- "best-in-class"
- "best of breed"
- "industry-leading"
- "revolutionary"
- "disruptive"
- "game-changer"
- "paradigm shift"
- "thought leader"
- "pain point"
- "value proposition"
- "customer journey"
- "seamless experience"
- "frictionless"
- "take it to the next level"

**Fresh alternatives:**
- Instead of "pain point" → name the specific problem
- Instead of "seamless experience" → describe what actually works smoothly
- Instead of "frictionless" → "users can do X in 2 clicks instead of 8"

### Product & Features
**Overused:**
- "intuitive interface"
- "user-friendly"
- "easy to use"
- "powerful and flexible"
- "robust solution"
- "scalable architecture"
- "enterprise-grade"
- "mission-critical"
- "cutting-edge technology"
- "state-of-the-art"
- "feature-rich"
- "comprehensive solution"

**Fresh alternatives:**
- Instead of "intuitive interface" → show what specific actions are obvious
- Instead of "scalable" → "handles 10M requests/day"
- Instead of "enterprise-grade" → name the specific security/compliance features

### Sales & Pitches
**Overused:**
- "untapped market opportunity"
- "exponential growth potential"
- "first-mover advantage"
- "sustainable competitive advantage"
- "strategic partnership"
- "win-win situation"
- "value-add"
- "circle back"
- "touch base"
- "reach out"
- "hop on a call"

**Fresh alternatives:**
- Instead of "untapped market" → quantify the addressable market with bottom-up math
- Instead of "exponential growth" → show actual growth rates with data
- Instead of "hop on a call" → "talk Tuesday at 3pm"

## Technical Writing

### Architecture & Systems
**Overused:**
- "highly available"
- "fault-tolerant"
- "horizontally scalable"
- "loosely coupled"
- "microservices architecture"
- "event-driven"
- "real-time processing"
- "big data"
- "machine learning powered"
- "AI-driven"
- "cloud-native"

**Fresh alternatives:**
- Instead of "highly available" → "99.95% uptime SLA"
- Instead of "real-time" → "sub-100ms latency"
- Instead of "machine learning powered" → name the specific model/algorithm

### Code & Implementation
**Overused:**
- "elegant solution"
- "clean code"
- "optimized algorithm"
- "efficient implementation"
- "performant"
- "idiomatic"
- "production-ready"
- "battle-tested"
- "bulletproof"

**Fresh alternatives:**
- Instead of "elegant solution" → explain why it's simple/maintainable
- Instead of "optimized" → "reduced from O(n²) to O(n log n)"
- Instead of "production-ready" → list specific tests/monitoring in place

### Documentation
**Overused:**
- "simply"
- "just"
- "easy"
- "straightforward"
- "obviously"
- "clearly"
- "of course"
- "as you can see"
- "note that"
- "keep in mind"

**Fresh alternatives:**
- Delete most of these—they're filler
- If something is actually complex, say "this requires careful attention to X"
- If simple, let the simplicity speak for itself

## Creative Writing

### Fiction & Storytelling
**Overused:**
- "a rollercoaster of emotions"
- "time seemed to stand still"
- "her heart skipped a beat"
- "butterflies in their stomach"
- "eyes like windows to the soul"
- "little did they know"
- "against all odds"
- "fate had other plans"
- "in the blink of an eye"
- "there are two types of people"

**Fresh alternatives:**
- Replace emotional cliches with specific physical reactions
- Replace time cliches with concrete details that show passage
- Replace fate/destiny language with character decisions

### Descriptive Writing
**Overused:**
- "breathtaking view"
- "picturesque landscape"
- "hustle and bustle"
- "quiet as a mouse"
- "dark as night"
- "white as snow"
- "tip of the iceberg"
- "needle in a haystack"
- "a stone's throw away"

**Fresh alternatives:**
- Replace with specific sensory details
- Name actual colors, sounds, distances
- Use fresh, context-specific comparisons

### Character & Dialogue
**Overused:**
- "strong female character"
- "complex anti-hero"
- "flawed but likeable"
- "coming of age"
- "finding themselves"
- "journey of self-discovery"
- "inner demons"
- "emotional baggage"

**Fresh alternatives:**
- Show character traits through actions, not labels
- Replace journey metaphors with specific conflicts
- Name the actual flaws and how they manifest

## Academic & Analytical

### Research & Analysis
**Overused:**
- "further research is needed"
- "it is worth noting that"
- "it is important to remember"
- "this paper explores"
- "this study investigates"
- "in recent years"
- "growing body of evidence"
- "preliminary findings suggest"
- "statistically significant"
- "correlation does not imply causation"

**Fresh alternatives:**
- Instead of "further research needed" → name specific open questions
- Instead of "it is worth noting" → just state it
- Instead of "growing body of evidence" → cite specific key studies

### Argumentation
**Overused:**
- "it could be argued that"
- "one might say"
- "some would contend"
- "critics argue"
- "proponents claim"
- "conventional wisdom holds"
- "on the one hand"
- "on the other hand"
- "in conclusion"

**Fresh alternatives:**
- Instead of "it could be argued" → just argue it
- Instead of "critics argue" → name specific critics and their actual arguments
- Instead of "in conclusion" → delete and just conclude

### Explanatory
**Overused:**
- "in other words"
- "that is to say"
- "put simply"
- "fundamentally"
- "essentially"
- "basically"
- "in essence"
- "at its core"
- "the key takeaway"

**Fresh alternatives:**
- Often these are filler—just make the clear statement
- If rephrasing needed, just rephrase without announcing it

- "now more than ever"

## Domain-Crossing Mega-Cliches

These appear everywhere and are instant AI tells:

### Metaphorical Cliches
- "journey" (for any process)
- "landscape" (business landscape, tech landscape)
- "ecosystem" (any interconnected system)
- "toolkit" (any set of resources)
- "roadmap" (any plan)
- "framework" (any structured approach)
- "lens" (any perspective)
- "fabric" (social fabric, organizational fabric)
- "tapestry" (any complex mixture)

**Replacement strategy:**
- Be literal instead of metaphorical
- If metaphor needed, use fresh, context-specific ones
- Often the metaphor is hiding vague thinking—be more precise

### Intensifiers & Hedges
**Overused intensifiers:**
- "very"
- "really"
- "quite"
- "extremely"
- "incredibly"
- "absolutely"
- "totally"
- "completely"

**Overused hedges:**
- "somewhat"
- "rather"
- "fairly"
- "relatively"
- "perhaps"
- "possibly"
- "potentially"
- "arguably"

**Fresh alternatives:**
- Delete intensifiers and find stronger base words
- Delete hedges and either commit or qualify specifically

## Detection Strategy

### Pattern Matching
1. Run exact string match for items in this list
2. Check for structural patterns (e.g., "[adjective]-driven" formulations)
3. Look for metaphor overuse (journey, landscape, ecosystem in same piece)

### Contextual Analysis
Not all matches are violations:
- Technical terms that happen to match (e.g., "machine learning" is fine in ML contexts)
- Domain-standard terminology (e.g., "API" in technical docs)
- Direct quotes that happen to contain cliches

### Replacement Quality Check
After replacing cliches:
1. Is replacement more specific?
2. Is replacement itself a cliche?
3. Does replacement improve clarity?
4. Does it sound natural in context?

## Common Replacement Mistakes

**Mistake: Synonym Swapping**
- Bad: "leverage" → "utilize" (equally generic)
- Good: "leverage" → "use" or specific action

**Mistake: Over-correction**
- Bad: Removing all familiar phrases makes writing awkward
- Good: Remove AI tells, keep natural language patterns

**Mistake: False Originality**
- Bad: Using weird metaphors just to be different
- Good: Being concrete and specific instead of metaphorical

**Mistake: Domain Confusion**
- Bad: Removing industry-standard terms because they're "overused"
- Good: Distinguish jargon (necessary) from cliche (unnecessary)

## Progressive Refinement

**First pass:** Catch obvious/universal cliches
- "game-changer," "leverage," "journey," "ecosystem"

**Second pass:** Domain-specific patterns
- Technical cliches in technical content
- Business cliches in strategy docs

**Third pass:** Subtle repetition
- Multiple uses of same metaphor family
- Reliance on same sentence structures
- Overuse of particular connecting words

**Final validation:**
- Read aloud—does it sound human?
- Compare to exemplar work in domain
- Check: would this pass as human-written to expert reader?
